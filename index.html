About us

United by faith, and the city we call home.

Indian Christian Fellowship Pittsburgh is a gathering of families and individuals from across the Indian subcontinent and diaspora — united by faith in Christ and by the simple desire to worship and break bread together.

We are not a denomination and we are not a church. We are a fellowship — a volunteer ministry that has met faithfully in the Pittsburgh area for over two decades. First-generation immigrants and second-generation Pittsburghers, students and retirees, lifelong believers and curious newcomers all sit at the same table.

What an evening looks like

Worship, the Word, and a shared meal.

We meet on the second Saturday of every month, beginning at 6:30 PM. A typical evening flows like this — followed by a big potluck dinner together.

  • 6:30 PMWorship togetherSongs in many languages — sing along in whichever feels like home.

  • 7:45 PMClosing prayer
  • 8:00 PMFellowship dinnerA potluck of Indian dishes — bring a dish if you can, come hungry if you can't.

Bring the whole family.

ICF gatherings are family gatherings. Children and youth have their own ministry running while the adult service is happening — songs, stories, and activities for every age. Then everyone comes together for dinner.

Expect laughter, a little chaos, and a community that watches out for one another's families.
