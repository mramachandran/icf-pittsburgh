About us

United by faith, and the city we call home.

Indian Christian Fellowship Pittsburgh is a gathering of families and individuals from across the Indian subcontinent and diaspora — united by faith in Christ and by the simple desire to worship and break bread together.

We are not a denomination and we are not a church. We are a fellowship — a volunteer ministry that has met faithfully in the Pittsburgh area for over two decades. First-generation immigrants and second-generation Pittsburghers, students and retirees, lifelong believers and curious newcomers all sit at the same table.

"Our gatherings are family gatherings. We have a big family dinner together."

What an evening looks like

Worship, the Word, and a shared meal.

We meet on the second Saturday of every month, beginning at 6:30 PM. A typical evening flows like this — followed by a big potluck dinner together.

  • 6:30 PM Worship together Songs in many languages — sing along in whichever feels like home.
  • 6:45 PM Welcome & announcements A short moment to greet newcomers and share community news.
  • 7:00 PM The Word A message from a pastor or guest speaker — often invited from churches across the region.
  • 7:45 PM Closing prayer
  • 8:00 PM Fellowship dinner A potluck of Indian dishes — bring a dish if you can, come hungry if you can't.

Bring the whole family.

ICF gatherings are family gatherings. Children and youth have their own ministry running while the adult service is happening — songs, stories, and activities for every age. Then everyone comes together for dinner.

Expect laughter, a little chaos, and a community that watches out for one another's families.

Through the year

A rhythm of worship, food, and celebration.

Beyond the monthly gathering, a few seasons of the year bring something special.

Every month

Saturday Fellowship

Our anchor — second Saturday of every month at 6:30 PM. Worship, a message, prayer, and a fellowship dinner. All are welcome.

December

Christmas Program

A festive evening of carols, songs, and music as our families come together to celebrate the birth of our Savior. Everyone is invited to share a song or a piece of music.

August

Summer Picnic

Once a year, we trade the church hall for a park — outdoor worship, games for the kids, and a long, easy afternoon of food and friendship.

Throughout the year

Guest Speakers & Youth Nights

We regularly invite pastors and speakers from across the Pittsburgh region to share God's Word, and partner with other ministries on multi-lingual youth worship nights.
